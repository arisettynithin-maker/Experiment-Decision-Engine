# Metric Framework

## Primary Decision Metric

**Purchase conversion rate**

Definition:
Orders / eligible exposed users

Reason:
This is the clearest measure of whether the banner improves purchase completion among the target segment.

## Secondary Metrics

### Average Order Value

Used to understand whether conversion lift is accompanied by order value compression or expansion.

### Add-to-Cart Rate

Used as an upper-funnel signal to detect whether the banner influences purchase intent before checkout.

### Checkout Start Rate

Used to isolate whether the message improves progression into the conversion funnel.

## Guardrail Metrics

### Gross Margin Per Order

Do not accept conversion lift if gross margin materially declines beyond the pre-agreed threshold.

### Refund Or Cancellation Rate

Detect negative downstream effects caused by misleading expectations or poor targeting.

### Delivery Subsidy Cost Per Order

Ensure the treatment does not create an uneconomic gain in orders.

## Success Thresholds

- Primary metric shows positive lift with rough statistical confidence
- Margin decline remains within threshold
- Refund or cancellation rate remains stable
- Delivery subsidy cost remains commercially acceptable
